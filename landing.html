About me
I'm Hana and I'm a

A literary major, journalist and writer with an affinity for all things tech. Earned my bachelor degree in English Literature in my home country Lebanon and worked as a journalist for over 10 years between Beirut and Dubai. Over the course of my career which was mainly print and later on extended into digital, I was very interested in how information was curated and distributed. I often found myself drawn to the technical side of things, learning html and working with different CMS platforms. Developing those skills came in handy but remained very curious about what lays under the hood. After my long journalistic stint, I was on sabbatical where I backpacked through Asia to do my own version of “Eat, Pray, Love” after a very satisfying career. It was during that trip that I found my way back to Academia which led me to Belgium where I joined KU Leuven for a Masters in Western Literature. During the last semester of my program, I fell on the Digital Humanities program which I am following now. This program has opened my eyes and rekindled my curiosity and my love for digital and all things tech. From scripting languages and web development, to data visualizations and AI, everyday I am introduced to new ways I can become a better humanities graduate, journalist and writer in the digital age we live in where data has become gold. While the future direction of this blog is still very much up in the air, I thank you for taking the time to browsing those pages and I invite you to visit my GitHub page and open new issues and try out my code .
